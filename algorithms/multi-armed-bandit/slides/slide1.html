Slide 1: Problem Formulation

Mathematical Setup

  • K-armed bandit: K actions (arms) to choose from
  • Each action a: Has a true value q_*(a) = \mathbb{E}[R_t | A_t = a]
  • Goal: Maximize total reward over T time steps
  • Regret: The difference between optimal and actual performance

Expected Regret:

L_T = T \cdot \mathbb{E}[R | a^*] - \sum_{t=1}^{T} \mathbb{E}[R_t]

where a^* is the optimal action

Action-Value Estimate:

Q_t(a) = \frac{\text{sum of rewards when } a \text{ taken prior to } t}{\text{number of times } a \text{ taken prior to } t}
Q_t(a) = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{t-1} R_i \cdot \mathbb{1}_{A_i = a}}{\sum_{i=1}^{t-1} \mathbb{1}_{A_i = a}}

Question: Why is regret a better metric than just total reward?

Answer: Because regret normalizes performance against the optimal strategy, making it easier to compare algorithms across different problem settings. It measures the "cost of learning."

Key Insight: The regret L_T grows with time, but the rate of growth is what matters. Good algorithms achieve O(\log T) regret (logarithmic), while naive exploration suffers O(T) linear regret.
